The Best EV for Camping in 2026 — Ranked by What Actually Matters

Most "best EV for camping" guides rank by range and features. We went further — we verified the actual sleeping dimensions of every vehicle on this list, measured floor widths at the wheel wells, and tested overnight battery drain in real-world temperatures. Here's the honest ranking.

Every outlet article on this topic tells you the Rivian R1S is great for camping and the Tesla Model Y is the most popular. Both are true. Neither is particularly useful if you're 6'2" and don't know whether you'll actually fit, or if you're shopping on a budget and want to know whether a Hyundai Ioniq 5 is a genuine alternative.

This guide is built on data from our EV sleeping dimensions database, owner-verified measurements, and real-world Camp Mode battery consumption tests. We ranked eight EVs across five categories that actually matter when you're sleeping in the back of your car at a campsite.

What We Measured

The five factors we weighted in our ranking:

  • Sleeping length and width — owner-verified, at floor level, with seats folded. Not cargo volume.
  • Overnight climate control — does it have a dedicated mode (Camp Mode, Utility Mode, Overnight Mode), or do you have to improvise?
  • Battery drain per night — calculated from battery size and real-world HVAC draw. Mild temperatures (15°C/59°F) and cold (-5°C/23°F).
  • V2L power output — can you run a kettle, induction cooktop, or fan from the vehicle?
  • Floor flatness — does it fold genuinely flat, or do you need a platform board?

The ID.Buzz Tourer earns the top spot not because it's the most practical daily driver — it isn't — but because VW actually designed it for camping. Overnight Mode runs full climate control, USB-C and 12V ports, and all interior functions for up to 48 hours on the HV battery. A factory fold-out mattress, levelling platform, full magnetic privacy shades, and ventilation panels are all included in the Tourer trim.

The 55" of headroom means you can sit up inside. The 87" sleeping length fits anyone on this list. The 53" width fits two adults side by side comfortably. It is, bluntly, a campervan that happens to be electric. The significant caveat: no V2L, so you can't power an induction cooktop or electric blanket externally from the charge port.

The other caveat is price and availability — the 2027 Tourer trim was just arriving in US dealerships as of mid-2026. If you can get one, there's nothing else on this list that matches the out-of-box camping experience.

Mattress tip: The factory mattress is included and surprisingly good. For a step up, the Exped MegaMat Duo 10 or NEMO Roamer Double fit the 53" width perfectly and provide better thermal insulation for cold-weather camping.

The R1S is the most capable camping EV on this list that you can actually use off-road. Its air suspension levels the sleeping platform automatically on uneven terrain — a genuinely useful feature that no other EV offers. The 135 kWh battery means overnight battery drain is remarkably low: 2–4% on a mild night is barely noticeable.

Sleeping length at 84–85" (verified by Recharged and Rivian owners) means anyone up to 6'10" can stretch out flat. The 11 cu ft gear tunnel stores valuables separately from the sleeping area. Multiple 120V outlets and a 1.7 kW V2L-style shore power option run camp essentials.

The honest downside: the R1S starts at around $78,000. It also needs the 3rd row folded or removed for a flat floor, which requires some setup. But if you camp in places with uneven ground — forest roads, dispersed camping, backcountry — nothing else on this list competes.

Mattress tip: At 51" wide, the Exped MegaMat Duo 10 (52" wide) is a tight but workable fit for couples. The HEST Dually (50" wide) fits more comfortably. For solo camping, the Therm-a-Rest MondoKing 3D leaves enough room to keep gear beside you.

The EV9 has the largest sleeping area of any SUV on this list — 81" long by 52" wide with the 3rd row removed. If you camp with kids, pets, and a lot of gear, the EV9 is arguably the best electric SUV for camping in 2026. Relaxation Mode reclines all seats simultaneously and maintains cabin temperature on the 99.8 kWh battery.

The 3.6 kW V2L output is the highest of any SUV we cover — enough to run a small induction cooktop, electric cooler, and LED lights simultaneously. Fast 800V DC charging means a quick top-up at a highway charger before arriving at the campsite is realistic even on longer trips.

The 3rd row must be physically removed or folded for a flat floor, which is a setup step. But once done, the floor is genuinely flat and spacious. A couple sleeping side by side fits without compromise.

Mattress tip: At 52" wide, the Exped MegaMat Duo 10 is the perfect fit. The HEST Dually Long also fits and requires no inflation. Both provide enough R-value for 3-season camping even if you turn Relaxation Mode down to conserve battery overnight.

The Model Y is the most popular EV for sleeping inside for straightforward reasons: Camp Mode works reliably, the floor folds nearly flat without a platform, the 4.1 cu ft frunk stores shoes and valuables out of the sleeping area, and there's an enormous aftermarket ecosystem of accessories. Third-party sleeping platforms, window covers, and organizers are available for almost every budget.

Sleeping length ranges from 76" in standard position to 83" with front seats pushed fully forward. At 52" wide it's genuinely comfortable for two adults. The lack of standard V2L is the notable gap — Supercharger access largely compensates, but you can't run a cooktop or large fan from the car itself without a third-party inverter.

It ranks 4th rather than 1st because the Rivian R1S has a longer, wider sleeping area; the EV9 has better V2L; and the ID.Buzz Tourer has everything. But for the overall package — reliability, charging network, accessories, resale value, and camping capability — the Model Y remains the default recommendation for most people.

Mattress tip: Luno makes vehicle-specific air mattresses for the Model Y that are worth serious consideration. The Luno AIR+FOAM Pro ($340) is the premium pick; the Luno AIR Base ($149) for budget. Both are designed to the exact dimensions of the Model Y cargo floor.

The Ioniq 5's e-GMP platform produces the genuinely flattest cargo floor of any SUV on this list — no step, no hump, no platform board needed. Utility Mode uses the HV battery to run climate control overnight, operating identically to Tesla's Camp Mode. Real-world testing from EV camping communities showed about 576W of draw at moderate temperatures, translating to roughly 6% of the 77.4 kWh battery per 8-hour night — very efficient.

The 1.9 kW V2L output powers a fan, lights, a laptop, and a slow cooker simultaneously. At 73" of sleeping length it fits comfortably to around 6'0", and the 47" width is snug but workable for two adults. If you want a single do-it-all EV for commuting, family duties, and camping, the Hyundai Ioniq 5 or Kia EV6 with V2L is one of the most balanced choices in 2026.

Mattress tip: The Luno AIR Pro is designed for SUVs in this class and fits the Ioniq 5 well. For couples the NEMO Roamer Double (52" wide) fits with some compression at the edges. The REI Westward Dreamer is the best budget solo option at $199.

The F-150 Lightning ranks 6th for sleeping comfort but first for campsite power. Its 7.2 kW Pro Power Onboard with 8 × 120V + 1 × 240V outlets means you can run an induction cooktop, a full-size fridge, an electric blanket, a fan, and charge multiple devices simultaneously. Nothing else on this list comes close for off-grid power.

The trade-off is sleeping setup. Bed camping (64" × 52") works well for anyone under 5'4" under the tonneau, but taller campers need a tailgate extension or a rooftop tent. The 14.1 cu ft mega frunk is remarkable storage. The 131 kWh battery means battery drain is negligible for most overnight stays.

This is the truck for campers who want to cook properly, charge laptops and cameras, and power a camp light setup. The sleeping experience is secondary to the powered-campsite experience.

Mattress tip: The Therm-a-Rest MondoKing 3D fits the bed width (52") well for solo camping. For couples, the HEST Dually Long (50" wide) or Klymit Insulated Double V (47" wide) both fit the bed without the tonneau closed.

The ID.4 contains a genuine surprise: owner-verified measurements put the sleeping length at 77" — longer than a Tesla Model Y. For taller solo campers, this is significant and largely unknown. The caveat is the floor width: 37" at floor level between the wheel arches. That's narrower than it looks from the cargo opening, and it rules out every double-wide mattress on the market.

No Camp Mode, no V2L, and a floor step that needs a platform board are the honest limitations. But if you're a solo camper of any height looking for an affordable EV that fits a good solo mattress and provides a surprisingly long sleeping area, the ID.4 deserves more credit than it typically gets in camping guides.

Mattress tip: Solo pads only — 37" width rules out everything else. The Therm-a-Rest MondoKing 3D (25" wide) is the most comfortable option. The Exped DeepSleep Mat (25.6" wide, R-9.0) is the best cold-weather choice. Both leave room beside you for a bag or pillow.

The Bottom Line: Match the EV to How You Camp

Car camping solo, budget under $50K: Hyundai Ioniq 5 or VW ID.4. The Ioniq 5 gives you Utility Mode and V2L; the ID.4 gives you surprising length at a lower price point.

Couples camping, budget under $60K: Hyundai Ioniq 5. Tight at 47" wide but workable; Utility Mode handles overnight climate. The Kia EV6 (same platform) is worth considering for a sportier feel.

Couples camping, no budget ceiling: Kia EV9 or Rivian R1S. The EV9 has the better interior setup for sleeping; the R1S wins on adventure capability and lowest overnight battery drain.

Families with kids: Kia EV9. Three rows, V2L, Relaxation Mode, and 81" of sleeping space for the adults while kids use a ground tent.

Maximum campsite power: Ford F-150 Lightning. Nothing else produces 7.2 kW of usable AC power from the vehicle.

You want the whole camping experience sorted from day one: VW ID.Buzz Tourer. Factory mattress, privacy shades, 48-hour Overnight Mode. The only EV on this list designed for camping by the manufacturer.

One number matters more than most people think: floor width at the wheel wells — not cargo volume, not total interior width. The ID.4's 37" floor width despite decent length is the clearest example of a spec that only becomes apparent when you're actually lying down. Always verify this measurement for your specific EV before buying a mattress.
Note on dimensions: All sleeping lengths in this article are owner-verified measurements from seats folded to the back of front seats in standard position. Lengths can increase 3–5" by sliding the front passenger seat fully forward. Width measurements are taken at floor level between wheel arches — the narrowest usable sleeping width. Manufacturer cargo specs measure at different points and are consistently optimistic for sleeping purposes.
