About This Guide

EV camping deserves better data than generic gear sites provide.

Why We Built This

When we started camping in EVs, we noticed that mainstream mattress reviews don't address range impact, EV-specific fitment, or how Camp Mode changes insulation needs. Traditional guides tell you to buy the warmest, heaviest mattress. For EV campers, that advice is often wrong.

This guide focuses exclusively on what EV owners actually need: lightweight options, fitment specs by model, and insulation guidance optimized for vehicles with climate control.

Our Data Sources

  • Manufacturer websites (Luno, Exped, Therm-a-Rest, NEMO, Sea to Summit, Klymit)
  • Trusted review sources (OutdoorGearLab, Switchback Travel, CleverHiker, GearJunkie, BetterTrail)
  • REI and Amazon for pricing verification and user reviews
  • EPA efficiency data for range calculations
  • EV owner communities (Reddit: r/TeslaLounge, r/Ioniq5, r/electricvehicles)

Range Impact Methodology

We use the industry-standard formula: 100 lbs additional weight ≈ 1% range reduction. This is derived from EPA testing and real-world EV owner data. It accounts for rolling resistance and minor aerodynamic effects of interior cargo.

Note: roof-mounted cargo has additional aerodynamic drag beyond just weight (typically 10-15% extra loss at highway speeds). We calculate mattress weight impact only — not roof box drag.
